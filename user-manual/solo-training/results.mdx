# Solo Training Results

Analyze your solo training performance to identify strengths, gaps, and areas for improvement.

## Accessing Your Results

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete Scenario">
    Finish all decisions in the scenario
  </Step>

  <Step title="Results Screen Appears">
    Automatic transition to results
  </Step>

  <Step title="View Summary">
    See overall score and highlights
  </Step>

  <Step title="Explore Details">
    Click for decision-by-decision breakdown
  </Step>

  <Step title="Access Later">
    Find in **Solo Dashboard** > **My History**
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Score Breakdown

Your score comes from:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Decision Quality">
    **60-70% of total**

    - Points for each decision
    - Weighted by importance
    - Optimal choices earn full points
    - Suboptimal earn partial credit
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Resource Usage">
    **10-20% of total**

    - Accessed relevant documents
    - Used information effectively
    - Didn't miss critical resources
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Scenario Completion">
    **10-20% of total**

    - All decisions made
    - Finished scenario
    - Engaged with all phases
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Bonus Points">
    **0-10% extra**

    - Exceptional decisions
    - Creative problem-solving
    - Advanced techniques applied
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Decision Analysis

### Individual Decision Review

For each decision, see:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Your Choice">
    - Option you selected
    - Points earned
    - Percentage of optimal
    - When you decided
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Feedback">
    - Why choice scored as it did
    - Consequences of your decision
    - Principle or framework illustrated
    - What information supported/contradicted it
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Optimal Choice">
    - Highest-scoring option
    - Why it was best
    - Points it would have earned
    - Reasoning behind it
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="All Options">
    - Every available choice
    - Score for each
    - Brief explanation of each outcome
    - Learning from alternatives
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Decision Patterns

Analysis of your decision-making style:

**Risk Profile**:
- Conservative: Favor safe, proven approaches
- Balanced: Mix of caution and boldness
- Aggressive: Willing to take chances

**Decision Speed**:
- Deliberate: Take time to analyze
- Moderate: Balance speed and thoroughness
- Quick: Decide rapidly (good or bad depending on accuracy)

**Information Usage**:
- Research-Heavy: Consult many resources
- Balanced: Selective resource use
- Intuition-Led: Rely more on judgment

## Comparing Performance

### Personal Benchmarks

Track your progress:

- **First Attempt vs. Retry**: Measure improvement
- **Beginner vs. Advanced**: Growth over time
- **By Category**: Where you're strongest/weakest
- **Trending**: Getting better or plateauing?

### Anonymous Benchmarks

(If enabled by organization)

See how you compare to:

- **All Users**: Overall platform average
- **Your Organization**: Company average
- **Peer Group**: Similar role/experience level
- **Top Performers**: 90th percentile

<Note>
Comparisons are anonymous. Others can't see your individual scores unless you choose to share.
</Note>

## Using Results for Improvement

### Reflection Questions

After reviewing results, ask yourself:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What Surprised Me?">
    - Decisions I thought were good but scored poorly
    - Unexpected consequences
    - Information I missed
    - Alternative approaches I didn't consider
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What Patterns Do I See?">
    - Consistent strengths
    - Recurring mistakes
    - Tendencies in my decision-making
    - Types of decisions I find challenging
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What Will I Apply?">
    - Key lessons learned
    - Principles to remember
    - Approaches to use in real crises
    - Skills to develop further
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How Will I Improve?">
    - Areas to focus practice on

    - Scenarios to retry or attempt
    - Real-world application opportunities

</AccordionGroup>
