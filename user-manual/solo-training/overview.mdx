# Solo Training Overview

Solo Training allows individuals to practice crisis management skills independently, at their own pace, with personalized AI guidance.

## Solo vs Group Simulations

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Solo Training">
    **Individual Focus**

    - You alone manage the crisis
    - AI mentor provides guidance
    - Practice at your own pace
    - Retry as often as you like
    - Personal performance tracking
    - Ideal for: Skill building, practice, preparation
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Group Simulations">
    **Team Collaboration**

    - Work with teammates
    - Real-time coordination required
    - Scheduled start times
    - Single attempt (usually)
    - Team and individual metrics
    - Ideal for: Team training, assessment, coordination practice
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Learning Modes

### Practice Mode

Focus on learning:

- Unlimited time for decisions
- Full AI mentor assistance
- Detailed feedback after each decision
- No scoring pressure
- Ideal for first attempts or skill building

### Challenge Mode

Test your skills:

- Timed decisions
- Limited AI hints
- Summary feedback (not after each decision)
- Score counts toward rankings
- Ideal for assessment or competition

### Exam Mode

Formal assessment:

- Strict timing
- No AI assistance
- Results sent to administrator
- Single attempt
- Used for certification or evaluation

## Solo Training Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start with Easier Scenarios">
    Build confidence and foundational skills before tackling advanced scenarios.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use the AI Mentor">
    Don't struggle in silence. The mentor is there to help you learn, not just test you.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Take Notes">
    Jot down insights and lessons as you go. They're valuable for real situations.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Review Feedback Carefully">
    Don't just look at scores. Read why decisions were scored as they were.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Retry Scenarios">
    Running through a scenario multiple times with different approaches deepens learning.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Apply Frameworks">
    Use crisis management frameworks you've learned. Practice makes them second nature.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Set Personal Goals">
    Work toward specific scores or skills rather than just completing scenarios.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
